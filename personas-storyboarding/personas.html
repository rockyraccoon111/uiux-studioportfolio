Personas and Storyboarding

The CVS machine checkout stations substitute for human checkout stations to reduce wait times and expedite the checkout process. I developed sketches of the interface and conducted interviews to produce personas and a storyboard.

Skills: Sketching (Balsamiq) User Interviews Personas Storyboarding

Part 1: Sketching

Sketches

First, I used Balsamiq to make some sketches representing a common flow using the CVS machine checkout station.

Key Interactive Components

The sketching process helped me realize some key interactive components:

  • Initial, bright red screen indicates machine availability to potential users
  • Once user begins interacting with checkout station, screen divided in 2 sections: receipt on left and available user actions on right
  • Once user journey is complete, machine automatically returns to initial availability state

Part 2: Observations and Interviews

Observations

Before I began interviewing, I took time to observe CVS customers engaging with the checkout machines. I noticed that:

  • Customers stand in line for machine checkouts even when human checkouts available
  • Various machine checkout issues requiring human cashier assistance (e.g. age restricted items)
  • Compared to human chechouts, machine checkouts are used for quicker purchases of fewer items

Interview Questions

I then began interviewing customers. I asked:

  • Why did you choose to come to this convenience store today?
  • How many times have you been to this CVS this past week?
  • Why do you use this checkout station instead of the human checkout station?
  • What is your favorite thing about this station?
  • What is your least favorite thing about this station?
  • What would you change about this station?

Interview Summary

From my interviews, I learned that CVS customers:

  • Go to this convenience store because it is closest to where they live (many are students)
  • Prefer machine checkout because believe it's faster
  • Like the minimal interaction with other humans (especially when buying sensitive items)
  • Dislike when human assistance necessary

Part 3: Personas

Persona 1: Private Paige

Paige is a freshman in college. It’s her first time living away from home, and when she buys products she feels are private, she worries about bumping into people she knows. Paige gets embarrassed and frustrated when the checkout machine requires the human cashier’s assistance. Since this CVS sells many personal care items and is located near freshman dorms, its machine checkout stations are often used by students like Paige.

    Thinks
  • I hope I don't bump into anyone I know!
  • I wish I didn't have to buy this so publicly
  • I wonder if my roommate's going to the party tonight?
    Feels
  • Stressed about adjusting to college life
  • Nervous about someone seeing her purchases
  • Anxious about her first college party
    Says
  • Whispers when asks employee, "Where can I find tampons?"
  • Avoids conversation as much as possible
    Does
  • Tries to cover her purchases from those around her
  • Quickly puts items away

Persona 2: Hurried Hector

Hector is a graduate student. Balancing his courses, research, social life, job search, and new relationship takes up a lot of his time, and he feels he’s always rushing to his next meeting. Hector gets frustrated when waiting in line and when the machine takes time processing its payment. Since many CVS customers are busy students who are there to purchase 1 or 2 items, there are many users like Hector who want to go through the checkout process as fast as possible.

    Thinks
  • I'm going to be late to my meeting
  • I wonder if CVS has cooking spices?
  • I wish checking out had fewer steps
    Feels
  • Stressed about upcoming job interviews
  • Excited to cook new recipe for girlfriend
  • Behind on his research work
    Says
  • "Do you mind if I go ahead of you? I'm running late for a meeting and only have one item"
  • "Sorry, excuse me"
    Does
  • Presses checkout screen with lots of force
  • Checks his watch often

Part 4: Storyboarding

Storyboard

Using my first persona, Private Paige, I then created a storyboard describing her user journey.

Reflection

Though I had had previous experience sketching and interviewing, this project revealed to me the benefits of creating personas and storyboards.
By representing my observations and interview responses as personas and then as a storyboard, I became a more attentive and empathetic UX designer.
